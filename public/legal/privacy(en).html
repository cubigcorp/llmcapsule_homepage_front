3. Personal Information Processing and Retention Period

The Company processes and retains personal information within the period permitted by law or the period agreed upon by the data subject at the time of collection.

The processing and retention periods for each type of personal information are as follows:

  • Website Membership Registration and Management: Until membership withdrawal. However, information required to be retained under relevant laws and regulations shall be retained according to the statutory retention period.
  • Goods or Service Provision: Until completion of goods/service supply and payment/settlement. However, usage records and behavioral information generated during service use for the purposes of preventing fraudulent use, handling complaints, and responding to litigation shall be retained for 5 years after membership withdrawal.
  • Inquiries and Complaints: Retained for 5 years after membership termination, or according to statutory retention periods for information required to be preserved under relevant laws and regulations.
  • Payment Settlement: Retained for 5 years after membership termination, or according to statutory retention periods for information required to be preserved under relevant laws and regulations.

8. Operation and Refusal of Automatic Personal Information Collection Devices (Cookies)

What is a Cookie?

The Company uses ‘cookies’ to store and periodically retrieve user information in order to provide personalized and customized services.

Cookies are very small text files sent by the server used to operate the website to the user's browser and are stored on the user's computer hard disk.

When the user subsequently visits the website, the website server reads the contents of the cookie stored on the user's hard disk to maintain the user's settings and provide customized services.

Cookies do not automatically or actively collect personally identifiable information, and users can refuse or delete these cookies at any time.

Purpose of Cookie Use

Cookies are used to understand the visit and usage patterns of each service and website visited by users, popular search terms, user scale, etc., to provide users with optimized, customized information, including advertisements.

Cookie Installation/Operation and Refusal

Users have the option to choose whether to install cookies. Therefore, users can set options in their web browser to allow all cookies, confirm each time a cookie is stored, or refuse to store all cookies.

However, refusing to store cookies may cause difficulties in using some of the company's services that require login.

The methods for specifying whether to allow cookie installation are as follows.

  • Internet Explorer: Select Tools menu > Select Internet Options > Click Privacy tab > Set privacy level
  • Chrome: Select Settings menu > Select Show advanced settings > Select Privacy and content settings > Set cookie level
  • Safari: Select Preferences menu > Select Privacy tab > Set cookie and website data level

9. Procedures and Methods for Disposing of Personal Information

The company promptly disposes of personal information once its retention period expires or its processing purpose is fulfilled. Electronic files are deleted using methods that prevent recovery, while paper documents are disposed of by shredding or incineration.

  • Reasons for destruction: Expiration of retention period, achievement of processing purpose, withdrawal of consent, etc.
  • Methods of destruction: Electronic files are deleted in a manner that prevents recovery; paper documents are shredded/incinerated
  • Partial destruction: Items requiring statutory retention after purpose fulfillment are stored separately and destroyed immediately upon expiration of the retention period

12. LLM Capsule Specialized Notice (On-Premises·Capsule Policy)

  • On-Premises Principle: Business data is processed and stored within the customer's infrastructure; the company does not access it by default.
  • External Integration Policy: When external LLM integration is required, only encapsulated (pseudonymized/masked) data is transmitted; original data is prohibited from external transmission by default.
  • Remote Support Access Control: For unavoidable remote support (e.g., troubleshooting), we adhere to the principles of prior approval, session logging, and least privilege.
  • Remote/Telemetry (Optional): Transmission of minimal diagnostic information (error codes, version, module status, etc.) for quality improvement is opt-in (consent-based) and does not include business data content.
